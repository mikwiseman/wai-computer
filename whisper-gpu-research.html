Owning the model instead of renting the API

Bottom line

Yes — running Whisper v3 on a rented GPU is trivial (it needs ~3 GB of VRAM). But the honest answer has three parts:

  • Don't run vanilla Whisper v3 for Russian — it's beaten ~3–7× by GigaAM v3 (MIT) and T-one (Apache); use Parakeet-v3 or whisper-large-v3-turbo for English. Route by language.
  • One cheap 24–32 GB card (L4, or the new RTX PRO 4500 Blackwell, ~$175–285/mo) serves ~1,000–3,000 daily users. VRAM is a non-issue; live-stream concurrency is the real limit.
  • Self-host for control and Russian quality — not price alone. Compute is ~12× cheaper than Deepgram, but Groq's hosted Whisper-turbo at $0.04/audio-hour beats self-hosting unless the GPU runs near 100%.

Fine-tuning: don't train our own first. Free Apache-2.0 Russian checkpoints already exist, and the best Russian model isn't even a Whisper fine-tune. A LoRA run is ~$20–55 of GPU; the real cost is labeled data ($15–45k for 200 hours).

02

Can we put Whisper v3 on a rented GPU?

Technically, easily. Whisper large-v3 is a 1.5B-parameter model; with faster-whisper (CTranslate2) at int8 it loads in ~3 GB (~5–6 GB with diarization). Any 24 GB card is oversized on memory — compute is the bottleneck, not VRAM. Two caveats:

  • Whisper has no true streaming mode (30-second windows). "Real-time Whisper" is a chunking trick — Silero VAD + faster-whisper, or WhisperLive. For low-latency dictation a natively-streaming model is cleaner.
  • For Russian, vanilla v3 is the wrong model — ~16–19% WER on real noisy Russian, vs ~3–5% for purpose-built open models. Details next.

The economics, plainly

A self-hosted L4 transcribes for roughly $0.0004/min of compute — about 12–19× cheaper than Deepgram ($0.0048–0.0077/min). But it only undercuts Groq's $0.04/audio-hour when pinned near 100% (~10 audio-hours per wall-clock hour). Below that, Groq is cheaper and zero-ops — so self-host for control, residency, and Russian quality, not raw savings, and keep Groq as the burst fallback.

Build the host · what to put the GPU in (RU)

A GPU is a PCIe-x16 card — it needs a host (motherboard + CPU + PSU + RAM + SSD + a case with airflow). For inference only the GPU/VRAM matters, so CPU/RAM/disk are cheapest-acceptable; the two things you must not skimp on are the PSU and case airflow. For the used Palit RTX 3090 GamingPro above (294 mm, 2.7-slot, 2× 8-pin, 350 W, hot GDDR6X) that means a 750–850 W Gold PSU with native PCIe cables and a mesh case that fits a ~300 mm card.

Where to put it: existing PC (₽0 if PSU ≥750 W Gold, space, and the right PCIe power cables) — cheapest; build from scratch (above) — most predictable; build-to-order — put the parts above through the DNS configurator + assembly/test service (~₽3–10k), or buy a prebuilt iGPU system unit with an 800 W PSU (~₽80k) and drop the 3090 in — assembled + warrantied, no soldering; server cards (Tesla/A10, passive) need a server case or a 3D-printed blower — won't cool in a quiet desktop; ready AI station (HYPERPC, DigitalRazor) — turnkey but pricier and bundles its own GPU; mini-PC / NUC — won't work (no full PCIe x16 or power; eGPU over Thunderbolt throttles).

Turnkey AI appliance? Not for ASR. NVIDIA's DGX Spark (GB10, 128 GB unified, $4,699 ≈ ₽430k+) and its clones (ASUS Ascent GX10, Dell, MSI EdgeXpert) are built for capacity — holding up to 200B-param models for local dev/fine-tune. Their memory bandwidth is only 273 GB/s — lower than a 5060 Ti (448) and 3.4× slower than a 3090 (936). ASR is bandwidth-bound, so for transcription a $4.7k Spark is slower than your ₽60–65k used 3090. Buy one to prototype big LLMs, never to serve Whisper. Likewise a ready gaming PC (NIX/DigitalRazor, ₽255–360k) bundles a GPU you don't need — for this build, bare 3090 + a cheap assembled host beats it.

Can we fine-tune it for Russian — and should we?

Base Whisper v3 sits at ~7–11% Russian WER on clean speech and ~16–19% on noisy/far-field. Fine-tuning helps — but the work is already done and published free, and the best Russian model isn't a Whisper fine-tune at all.

07

Recommended architecture

  • Language router up front → split Russian vs English at the session.
  • Russian: GigaAM v3 (RNNT for files, CTC for live); T-one for telephony-grade streaming.
  • English: Parakeet-tdt-0.6b-v3 for fast batch (or Granite Speech 4.1 for top accuracy), whisper-large-v3-turbo + Silero VAD for dictation.
  • Long recordings & meetings: VibeVoice-ASR (Microsoft, MIT, 9B) — 60-min single-pass ASR + diarization + timestamps, incl. Russian; the best open fit for our recordings use case.
  • Hardware: start on one L4 or RTX PRO 4500 Blackwell (32 GB) (~$175–285/mo) — headroom for ~1,000–3,000 daily users. Hetzner GEX44 (€184/mo fixed) to keep it on the existing backend.
  • Managed fallback: keep Groq Whisper-turbo ($0.04/audio-hr) wired in for overflow.
  • Serving: faster-whisper for the Whisper path; NeMo + Triton/TensorRT for GigaAM / T-one / Parakeet.
One honest caveat

This trades a per-minute vendor bill for an always-on GPU plus ops, model-serving, and on-call. At low/spiky volume, managed APIs (Groq especially) win on total cost. The case for self-hosting is Russian accuracy we can't buy off-the-shelf, data control, and predictable cost at scale — not a smaller invoice on day one.
